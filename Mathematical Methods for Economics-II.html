Core Course 4: Mathematical Methods for Economics-II

This course builds upon MME-I, introducing multi-variable calculus and linear algebra, essential for advanced economic modeling and optimization.

Course Content Details

1. Functions of Several Real Variables

This unit extends the concept of functions to cases involving multiple independent variables, which is common in economics (e.g., utility depending on quantities of several goods). We will explore how to visualize these functions and introduce the concept of partial derivatives to measure the rate of change with respect to one variable while holding others constant.

Key Topics:

  • Geometric Representation: Level Curves and Level Surfaces
  • Partial Derivatives and their Economic Interpretation
  • The Total Differential
  • Homogeneous Functions and Euler's Theorem

2. Multi-variable Optimization (Unconstrained and Constrained)

This is a core unit that applies multi-variable calculus to solve complex economic optimization problems. We will first examine unconstrained optimization. Then, we introduce the more realistic scenario of constrained optimization, using the Lagrangian multiplier method. This technique is fundamental for analyzing consumer choice subject to a budget constraint and firm decisions subject to cost constraints.

Key Topics:

  • First and Second-Order Conditions for Unconstrained Optimization
  • Constrained Optimization and the Method of Lagrange Multipliers
  • Interpretation of the Lagrange Multiplier (Shadow Price)
  • Applications: Utility Maximization and Cost Minimization

3. Linear Algebra: Vectors and Matrices

This unit introduces the tools of linear algebra, which provide a powerful and concise way to represent and solve systems of equations. We will cover vectors and matrices, and the various operations that can be performed on them. This is essential for handling large-scale economic models, econometrics, and input-output analysis.

Key Topics:

  • Vectors: Geometric and Algebraic Representation
  • Matrix Operations: Addition, Subtraction, and Multiplication
  • The Transpose and Inverse of a Matrix
  • Determinants and their Properties

4. Systems of Linear Equations

This unit applies the matrix algebra tools to solve systems of linear equations, which frequently appear in economic models (e.g., finding the equilibrium in a multi-market model). We will learn systematic methods like Cramer's Rule and matrix inversion to find unique solutions to these systems.

Key Topics:

  • Representing Economic Models in Matrix Form
  • Solving Systems of Linear Equations using Matrix Inversion
  • Cramer's Rule and its Applications
  • Leontief Input-Output Model

5. Integration and Differential Equations

The final unit introduces integral calculus and differential equations. Integration, the reverse of differentiation, is used to find total values from marginal values (e.g., total cost from marginal cost). Differential equations, which relate a function to its derivatives, are fundamental for analyzing the dynamic behavior of economic variables over time.

Key Topics:

  • Indefinite and Definite Integrals
  • Applications: Finding Total Cost, Consumer and Producer Surplus
  • First-Order Differential Equations
  • Applications in Economic Dynamics (e.g., Solow Growth Model)
