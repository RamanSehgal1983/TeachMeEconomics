Core Course 14: Development Economics-II

This course explores key topics in micro-development, including health, education, and finance, as well as the role of international trade and aid.

Course Content Details

1. Micro-foundations of Development: Health, Education, and the Household

This unit applies microeconomic tools to understand key aspects of human development. It examines the determinants of health and education outcomes in developing countries and analyzes the household as a decision-making unit. Topics include the economics of fertility, the critical importance of nutrition, and the link between education and economic growth. The role of gender and intra-household inequality in shaping development outcomes is also a central theme.

Key Topics:

  • The Demand for Health and Education
  • The Economics of the Household and Fertility Decisions
  • Nutrition and its Impact on Productivity
  • Gender Inequality and its Development Consequences

2. Land, Labor, and Credit Markets

This unit examines the functioning of key factor markets in developing countries, which often operate very differently from their counterparts in developed economies. It analyzes different land tenancy arrangements (like sharecropping), the functioning of rural labor markets, and the characteristics of credit markets, including the prevalence of informal lending and the role of microfinance. The focus is on understanding market imperfections and their implications for efficiency and equity.

Key Topics:

  • The Economics of Sharecropping and Land Tenancy
  • Rural Labor Markets and disguised unemployment
  • Credit Markets: Rationing, Collateral, and Informal Lending
  • Microfinance and its Impact

3. International Trade and Development

This unit explores the complex relationship between international trade and economic development. It reviews the traditional arguments for free trade based on comparative advantage and discusses alternative perspectives, such as the Prebisch-Singer hypothesis, which raises concerns about the terms of trade for developing countries. The debate between trade liberalization and protectionist strategies (like import substitution) as pathways to development is a central theme.

Key Topics:

  • Trade as an Engine of Growth: The Case for Free Trade
  • The Prebisch-Singer Hypothesis and Declining Terms of Trade
  • The Import-Substitution vs. Export-Promotion Debate
  • The Role of the WTO and Global Value Chains

4. Foreign Aid, Investment, and Debt

The final unit analyzes the role of international financial flows in the development process. It critically examines the effectiveness of foreign aid, discussing the debates surrounding its impact on growth and governance. The role of foreign direct investment (FDI) in bringing capital and technology is also explored. Finally, the unit covers the issue of external debt, including the causes and consequences of debt crises in the developing world.

Key Topics:

  • The Role and Effectiveness of Foreign Aid
  • Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) and Multinational Corporations (MNCs)
  • External Debt and Debt Crises
  • The Role of the IMF and the World Bank
