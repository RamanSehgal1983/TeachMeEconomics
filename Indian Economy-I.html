Core Course 11: Indian Economy-I

This course examines the historical context and key policy debates that have shaped the Indian economy from the colonial era to the pre-reform period.

Course Content Details

1. Economic Development since Independence

This foundational unit provides a broad overview of the Indian economy at the time of independence and traces its evolution through the subsequent decades. It examines the structural characteristics of the economy—such as the dominance of agriculture, widespread poverty, and institutional weaknesses—and analyzes the key trends in national income, growth, and structural change. The focus is on understanding the legacy of colonialism and the challenges faced by the newly independent nation.

Key Topics:

  • State of the Economy on the Eve of Independence
  • Trends in National and Per Capita Income
  • Occupational Structure and Sectoral Distribution of GDP
  • Debates on Poverty and Inequality

2. Policy Frameworks: The Role of State and Market

This unit delves into the ideological and strategic underpinnings of India's development strategy. It critically evaluates the dominant paradigm of a state-led, centrally planned development model, embodied by the Five-Year Plans. The rationale for this approach, including market failure arguments and the goal of building a self-reliant industrial base, is explored. The discussion also covers the shift in thinking over time and the growing recognition of the market's role, setting the stage for the economic reforms of the 1990s.

Key Topics:

  • The Nehru-Mahalanobis Strategy of Development
  • Rationale and Objectives of the Five-Year Plans
  • The Role of the Public Sector and Industrial Policy Resolutions
  • Critiques of the Planning and Regulatory Framework

3. Agrarian Sector: Land Reforms and Green Revolution

Given agriculture's central role, this unit examines the two most significant policy interventions in this sector. It starts with an analysis of the various land reform measures undertaken after independence, such as the abolition of intermediaries, tenancy reforms, and land ceilings, and assesses their impact. The second part focuses on the Green Revolution—the adoption of new high-yielding variety (HYV) technology—and analyzes its profound effects on agricultural productivity, income distribution, and regional disparities.

Key Topics:

  • The Rationale and Objectives of Land Reforms
  • Impact and Limitations of Land Reform Policies
  • The Green Revolution: Technology and Strategy
  • Economic and Social Consequences of the Green Revolution

4. Industrial Sector: Strategy and Performance

This unit critically examines India's industrial development strategy during the planning era. It focuses on the policy of import-substituting industrialization (ISI), which aimed to foster domestic industries by protecting them from foreign competition through tariffs and quotas. The role of the public sector in establishing a capital goods base is analyzed. The performance of the industrial sector, including issues of low productivity, lack of competitiveness, and the "Hindu rate of growth," is critically evaluated.

Key Topics:

  • The Strategy of Import-Substituting Industrialization (ISI)
  • The Industrial Policy Resolutions (1948, 1956)
  • Role and Performance of the Public Sector Enterprises (PSEs)
  • Critiques of the Industrial Licensing System ('License Raj')
