Core Course 13: Indian Economy-II

This course focuses on the macroeconomic policies and performance of the Indian economy in the post-1991 reform era.

Course Content Details

1. Macroeconomic Policies and Their Impact

This unit analyzes the key macroeconomic policies adopted in India, particularly since the 1991 reforms. It covers the objectives and instruments of fiscal policy, including issues related to government debt and deficits. It also examines monetary policy, focusing on the role of the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) and the shift towards inflation targeting. The external sector policies, such as trade liberalization and management of the exchange rate, are also critically evaluated.

Key Topics:

  • Fiscal Policy: Deficits, Debt, and the FRBM Act
  • Monetary Policy: Objectives, Instruments, and the Role of RBI
  • Trade and Exchange Rate Policies
  • Financial Sector Reforms

2. The 1991 Reforms: Rationale and Consequences

This unit provides an in-depth analysis of the landmark 1991 economic reforms. It begins by examining the balance of payments crisis that precipitated the reforms. It then details the key components of the reform package, often summarized as Liberalization, Privatization, and Globalization (LPG). The consequences of these reforms on economic growth, employment, poverty, and inequality are discussed, providing a balanced view of the achievements and the remaining challenges.

Key Topics:

  • The Balance of Payments Crisis of 1991
  • Structural Adjustment Program: Liberalization, Privatization, Globalization (LPG)
  • Impact of Reforms on Growth and Stability
  • Debates on the Social Consequences of Reforms

3. Agriculture: Policy and Performance

This unit examines the performance and policy challenges of the Indian agricultural sector in the contemporary period. It analyzes trends in agricultural growth and productivity, and discusses key policy issues such as food security, the Public Distribution System (PDS), and agricultural pricing. The unit also explores the sources and impact of agrarian distress, and debates surrounding farm subsidies and the role of technology and investment in modernizing the sector.

Key Topics:

  • Trends in Agricultural Growth and Productivity
  • Food Security and the Public Distribution System (PDS)
  • Agricultural Pricing and Subsidies
  • Agrarian Distress and Farmer Suicides

4. Industry and Services Sector: Performance and Policies

This unit assesses the performance of India's industry and services sectors, particularly in the post-reform era. It analyzes the changing structure of Indian industry and debates issues related to manufacturing growth and competitiveness. A significant focus is on the remarkable growth of the services sector, often termed "India's services revolution." The role of foreign direct investment (FDI) and the challenges of creating sufficient employment in these sectors are also examined.

Key Topics:

  • Industrial Performance and De-industrialization Debates
  • The Rise of the Services Sector
  • Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) Policy
  • Labor Regulations and Employment Trends
