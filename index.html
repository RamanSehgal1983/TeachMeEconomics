Economics Honors Curriculum

Your structured guide to the three-year Economics Honors syllabus, designed to help you master the core concepts of economic science.

Year 1: The Foundation

The first year of the economics curriculum lays the essential groundwork for your journey into the world of economics. This foundational year is designed to build a solid base of understanding. You will be introduced to the core principles of microeconomics, focusing on individual decision-making, and macroeconomics, which examines the economy as a whole. Alongside this, you will develop the essential mathematical skills, including calculus and algebra, that are required for the rigorous economic analysis and quantitative modeling you will encounter throughout the syllabus.

Core Subjects:

Introductory Microeconomics

Studies the behavior of individual economic agents, like households and firms. It covers how they make decisions in markets for goods and services, the forces of supply and demand, consumer choice, and different market structures like perfect competition and monopoly.

Mathematical Methods for Economics-I

Introduces the fundamental mathematical tools used to model economic theories. This includes algebra, functions of a single variable, derivatives, and their direct applications, such as calculating elasticity and finding marginal revenue or cost from total functions.

Introductory Macroeconomics

Focuses on the economy as a whole, covering key topics like GDP, inflation, unemployment, and national economic policies. You will learn about national income accounting, the determination of aggregate output, and the basics of fiscal and monetary policy.

Mathematical Methods for Economics-II

Advances your mathematical skills with multi-variable calculus and linear algebra. This course covers partial derivatives, constrained optimization using Lagrangians, and matrix operations, which are all essential for more complex economic models and analysis.

Year 2: Building the Core

The second year of the economics syllabus is designed to deepen your understanding of core economic theory with a suite of intermediate-level courses. You will move beyond introductory concepts to explore more complex and formal models of consumer and firm behavior, as well as macroeconomic frameworks like the IS-LM model. A significant part of this year involves applying statistical methods to analyze economic data, providing you with the foundational skills for empirical research. This critical year sets the stage for advanced study and a potential career in economic analysis.

Core Subjects:

Intermediate Microeconomics-I

Provides a rigorous analysis of consumer choice using indifference curves and budget constraints, and firm behavior through cost minimization. It builds upon introductory concepts to formally derive demand curves and analyze the Slutsky equation.

Intermediate Macroeconomics-I

Delves into models of aggregate demand and supply, including the IS-LM framework for analyzing policy effects in a closed economy. It also introduces the open economy using the Mundell-Fleming model to understand exchange rates and trade balances.

Statistical Methods for Economics

Covers the principles of probability, random variables, and key distributions (like Normal and t-distributions). It then applies these concepts to sampling, creating confidence intervals, and hypothesis testing, which are essential for empirical analysis in econometrics.

Intermediate Microeconomics-II

Explores general equilibrium using the Edgeworth box, welfare economics and Pareto efficiency, and situations where markets fail. This includes analyzing externalities, public goods, and the consequences of asymmetric information like adverse selection and moral hazard.

Intermediate Macroeconomics-II

Examines the micro-foundations of macroeconomics, including consumption theories (e.g., Permanent Income Hypothesis) and investment. It focuses on long-run economic growth using the Solow model and analyzes the inflation-unemployment tradeoff via the Phillips Curve.

Introductory Econometrics

Teaches how to apply statistical methods to real-world economic data. You will learn to use the Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) regression model to test theories, estimate economic relationships, and interpret coefficients, as well as handle violations of model assumptions.

Year 3: Advanced Applications

The final year of the economics curriculum transitions from pure theory to a strong focus on applied economics. In this advanced stage, you will tackle specialized and complex topics relevant to real-world policy. This includes a deep dive into the intricacies of the Indian economy, analyzing its historical development and contemporary challenges. You will also explore the multifaceted challenges of economic development, studying theories of growth, poverty, and international trade. This year requires you to apply the full range of theoretical and empirical tools you've acquired throughout the economics honors syllabus.

Core Subjects:

Indian Economy-I

Analyzes the historical development of the Indian economy since independence. It covers the planning era, structural features, key policies like land reforms, the Green Revolution, and the import-substituting industrial strategy before the 1991 reforms.

Development Economics-I

Introduces theories of economic growth and underdevelopment, such as the Harrod-Domar and Lewis models. It also focuses on the critical issues of poverty and inequality, including their measurement (e.g., Gini coefficient) and the concept of poverty traps.

Indian Economy-II

Focuses on contemporary macroeconomic policies and performance of the Indian economy, especially after the 1991 LPG reforms. It analyzes the performance and challenges of the agricultural, industrial, and service sectors in the modern era.

Development Economics-II

Explores specific topics in micro-development economics. This includes the role of land, labor, and credit markets (including microfinance), and the economics of health and education. It also covers the impact of international trade and foreign aid on development.
